Who are we?

The Cooperative Transportation Association of Jerusalem was established by Jerusalemites, young and old, who live in the city, love the city, and want to make it a better place in which to live. The idea of founding a cooperative emerged out of the widening gap between the blossoming of the entertainment and cultural activities offered on Shabbat and the lack of accessibility to them. We want to change the current situation in which only people with cars, or with ample means, can enjoy their weekends. We want to change the situation in which young people take to the driving wheel after drinking because they have no alternative. We want to make the cultural events in the city more egalitarian, more accessible and safer. We want to create a community that travels together and make private transportation inclusive, cooperative and shared.

Why a cooperative?

The Cooperative Transportation Association of Jerusalem assists us in traveling on Saturdays. The cooperative will launch transportation that is private, for members only, on a cooperative basis. Together, we can serve the needs of our community. The cooperative will engage transportation services for the use of its members. The more members that join the cooperative, the more we can lower costs, add routes, and increase the frequency of the transportation.

Route and Timetable

Once we've reached our initial fundraising goal, we will launch the first Shabus route. It will run from Pisgat Zeev via the city center to Gilo and will also pass through Talpiyot, the German Colony, and Musrara. The transportation will run on Friday nights between 8 p.m. and 1 a.m., at a frequency of at least once an hour (depending on how much money we raise!).

If you want more routes or more frequent rides - tell us and contribute!

The routes and frequency may change depending on the decisions of the members of the cooperative.
